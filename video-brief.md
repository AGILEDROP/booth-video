# Looping booth video: creative brief

**For: Video designer / AI video tool**
**Format: Looping video for TV screen at booth, 16:9, no audio assumed (but can have subtle background music)**
**Total duration: ~3–4 minutes per loop**
**Scene count: 10 scenes, ~18–22 seconds each**

---

## Core design concept

### The persistent overlay (always visible)

The screen is split into two layers:

**Layer 1 — The persistent frame (always on screen, never changes between scenes)**

A strip along the bottom (roughly 15–18% of screen height) that contains:

- **Left side of strip:** arp.software logo + tagline "Custom logistics software you own"
- **Right side of strip:** Agiledrop logo + "agiledrop.com"
- **Center of strip:** A horizontal progress tracker — 10 small dots or segments, one for each scene. The current scene's dot is highlighted/filled. As scenes advance, the highlight moves. This gives viewers:
  - A sense of where they are in the loop
  - How many scenes there are
  - Anticipation for what's next (the next dot could show a tiny label or icon that hints at the upcoming topic)
- Each dot/segment has a **micro-label** underneath or on hover-state equivalent — just one or two words: "Problem", "eFTI", "AI", "TMS", "Emissions", "Reference", "Reference", "Reference", "Platform", "Team". This way, even a glance at the strip tells you the full story structure.
- A subtle **progress bar animation** fills the current segment over the scene's duration, so viewers can see how long until the next scene. Think YouTube chapter markers meets loading bar.

**Layer 2 — The scene content (upper ~82–85% of screen)**

This is where each scene's unique content lives. Each scene follows one of a few layout templates (described below), but the persistent strip at the bottom anchors everything.

### Scene transition concept

Scenes don't hard-cut. They **crossfade or slide horizontally** (like a slow carousel). The persistent bottom strip stays fixed — only the content area transitions. This reinforces that the strip is the anchor and the content rotates above it.

### Visual style notes for designer

- **Clean, modern, professional.** Not startup-flashy, not corporate-boring. Think: confident European tech company.
- **Color palette:** Defer to arp.software / Agiledrop brand guidelines, but the feel should be: dark backgrounds (navy/charcoal) with bright accent colors for data points and highlights. The mocked UI screenshots will pop against dark backgrounds.
- **Typography:** One strong sans-serif for headlines, one clean sans-serif for body. Numbers and statistics should be large and prominent.
- **No stock photos.** Use mocked UI screenshots, abstract data visualizations, simple iconography, and clean typography. If imagery is needed, use stylized/abstract representations of logistics (port cranes, containers, warehouse layouts) — geometric or blueprint-style, not photographic.
- **Motion:** Subtle and purposeful. Elements fade/slide in with stagger. Numbers can count up. Progress bar fills smoothly. No flashy 3D transitions.

### Scene 1: "The problem"
**Progress tracker label:** Problem
**Duration:** ~20 seconds

**Purpose:** Anyone glancing at this screen immediately understands the market problem. No company name needed yet — the overlay handles that.

**Layout:** Full-width content area. Large statistic on left (60% of width), supporting context on right (40%).

**Content:**

- **Hero stat (large, animated count-up):** "99% of transport documents in Europe are still on paper"
- **Supporting line:** "500 million paper CMR notes printed every year"
- **Second stat below:** "72% of logistics companies still dispatch with spreadsheets"
- **Bottom text (smaller):** "EU deadline for electronic freight documents: July 2027"

**Animation concept:**
- The 99% number counts up from 0 on scene entry
- Paper document icons subtly rain/fall in the background (abstract, not literal — think light geometric shapes drifting down)
- The July 2027 date pulses subtly or has a countdown-style treatment

**Why this works as an entry point:** Someone walking by sees a shocking stat and a deadline. Immediately understands: this industry has a problem, time is running out.

## Technical notes for the video maker

### Loop behavior
- Scene 10 transitions seamlessly back to Scene 1. No fade to black. No "end" frame. The persistent bottom strip stays continuous.
- The progress tracker dots at the bottom loop: after the last dot fills, the first dot begins filling again.

### Scene independence
- Test each scene in isolation: cover the bottom strip with your hand and ask "Does this screen make sense if I see nothing else?" Every scene must pass this test.
- There is no "welcome" or "intro" scene. There is no "thank you" or "outro" scene.
- The loop can be entered at any point.

### Text density
- Maximum ~30 words of readable text per scene (excluding the persistent strip).
- Headlines: 5–8 words max.
- Descriptions: 1 line each.
- Stats and numbers do the heavy lifting, not paragraphs.

### Mocked screenshots
- I will provide mocked screenshots for Scenes 3, 6, 7, and 8. These are white-label recreations of our actual work, adapted for the logistics context.
- The screenshots should look like real, functioning software — clean UI, realistic data, professional polish.
- For Scene 3 (AI dashboard): this should look like the arp.software AI module — agent list, activity log, confidence scores, review queue.

### Duration flexibility
- If the video runs shorter than 3 minutes or longer than 4 minutes, that's fine. The priority is that each scene has enough breathing room to be read and absorbed, but not so long that it feels static.
- A good test: can someone read all the text on screen in 12–15 seconds? If yes, the remaining 5–8 seconds are for animation and absorption.

### Resolution and format
- 4K (3840×2160) preferred for TV display
- 16:9 aspect ratio
- Export as loopable MP4 (seamless loop point between last and first frame)
- Consider also exporting individual scenes as separate files for potential reuse in other contexts
